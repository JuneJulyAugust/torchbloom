## 15.2.1 Analysis of GAN loss function

If we divide the two sums in the first line of equation 15.5 by the numbers I, J of real and generated samples, then the loss function can be written in terms of expectations:

$$
\begin{aligned}L[\phi]\quad&=\;-\frac{1}{J}\sum_{j=1}^{I}\left(\log\left[1-\mathrm{sig}[\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{x}_{j}^{*},\phi]]\right]\right)-\frac{1}{I}\sum_{i=1}^{I}\left(\log\left[\mathrm{sig}[\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{x}_{i},\phi]]\right]\right)\\&\approx\;-\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}^{*}}\left[\log\left[1-\mathrm{sig}[\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{x}^{*},\phi]]\right]\right]-\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}\left[\log\left[\mathrm{sig}[\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{x},\phi]]\right]\right]\\&=\;-\int Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})\log\left[1-\mathrm{sig}[\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{x}^{*},\phi]]\right]d\mathbf{x}^{*}-\int Pr(\mathbf{x})\log\left[\mathrm{sig}[\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{x},\phi]]\right]d\mathbf{x},\end{aligned}
\tag{15.6}
$$

where $Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})$ is the probability distribution over the generated samples, and $Pr(\mathbf{x})$ is the true probability distribution over the real examples.

When $I = J$, the optimal discriminator for an example $\tilde{\mathbf{x}}$ of unknown origin is:

$$
\begin{aligned}
Pr(\mathrm{real}|\tilde{\mathbf{x}})=\underset{}{\mathrm{sig}\left[\mathrm{f}[\tilde{\mathbf{x}},\phi]\right]}=\frac{Pr(\bar{\mathbf{x}}|\mathrm{real})}{Pr(\tilde{\mathbf{x}}|g e n e r a t e d)+Pr(\bar{\mathbf{x}}|\mathrm{real})}=\frac{Pr(\mathbf{x})}{Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})+Pr(\bar{\mathbf{x}})},
\end{aligned}
\tag{15.7}
$$

where on the right hand side, we evaluate  $\tilde{x}$  against the generated distribution  $Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})$  and the real distribution  $Pr(x)$ . Substituting into equation 15.6, we get:

$$
\begin{aligned}L[\phi]\;=\;&-\int Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})\log\Big[1-\mathrm{sig}[\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{x}^{*},\phi]]\Big]d\mathbf{x}^{*}-\int Pr(\mathbf{x})\log\Big[\mathrm{sig}[\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{x},\phi]]\Big]d\mathbf{x}\\=\;&-\int Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})\log\Bigg[1-\frac{Pr(\mathbf{x})}{Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})+Pr(\mathbf{x})}\Bigg]d\mathbf{x}^{*}-\int Pr(\mathbf{x})\log\Bigg[\frac{Pr(\mathbf{x})}{Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})+Pr(\mathbf{x})}\Bigg]d\mathbf{x}\\=\;&-\int Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})\log\Bigg[\frac{Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})}{Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})+Pr(\mathbf{x})}\Bigg]d\mathbf{x}^{*}-\int Pr(\mathbf{x})\log\Bigg[\frac{Pr(\mathbf{x})}{Pr(\mathbf{x}^{*})+Pr(\mathbf{x})}\Bigg]d\mathbf{x}.\end{aligned}
\tag{15.7}
$$
