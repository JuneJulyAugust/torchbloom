Problem 15.3 Consider computing the earth mover’s distance using linear programming in the primal form. The discrete distributions  $Pr(x=i)$  and  $q(x=j)$  are defined on x = 1, 2, 3, 4 and:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathbf{b}=\left[Pr(x=1),Pr(x=2),Pr(x=3),Pr(x=4),q(x=1),q(x=2),q(x=3),q(x=4)\right]^{T}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{15.18}
$$

Write out the contents of the  $8 \times 16$  matrix A. You may assume that the contents of P have been vectorized into p column-first.

Problem 15.4 $^{*}$  Calculate (i) the KL divergence, (ii) the reverse KL divergence,(iii) the Jensen-Shannon divergence, and (iv) the Wasserstein distance between the distributions:

$$
\begin{aligned}
Pr(z)=\begin{cases}0&z<0\\1&0\leq z\leq1,\\0&z>1\end{cases}\quad and\quad Pr(z)=\begin{cases}0&z<a\\1&a\leq z\leq a+1.\\0&z>a\end{cases}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{15.19}
$$

for the range $a \in [-3, 3]$. To get a formula for the Wasserstein distance for this special case, consider the total “earth” (i.e., probability mass) that must be moved and multiply this by the distance it must move.

Problem 15.5 The KL distance and Wasserstein distances between univariate Gaussian distributions are given by:

$$
\begin{aligned}
D_{kl}=\log\left[\frac{\sigma_{2}}{\sigma_{1}}\right]+\frac{\sigma_{1}^{2}+(\mu_{1}-\mu_{2})^{2}}{2\sigma_{2}^{2}}-\frac{1}{2},
\end{aligned}
\tag{15.20}
$$

and

$$
\begin{aligned}
D_{w}=(\mu_{1}-\mu_{2})^{2}+\sigma_{1}+\sigma_{2}-2\sqrt{\sigma_{1}\sigma_{2}},
\end{aligned}
\tag{15.21}
$$

respectively. Plot these distances as a function of $\mu_{1}-\mu_{2}$ for the case when $o_{r1}=0$.
