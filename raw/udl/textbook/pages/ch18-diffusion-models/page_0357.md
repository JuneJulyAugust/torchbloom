## 18.4 Training

The joint distribution of the observed variable x and the latent variables {z_{t}} is: 

$$
P r(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})=P r(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}_{1},\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1})\prod_{t=2}^{T}P r(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}|\mathbf{z}_{t},\boldsymbol{\phi}_{t})\cdot P r(\mathbf{z}_{T}). \quad (18.17)
$$

 The likelihood of the observed data $Pr(\mathbf{x}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})$ is found by marginalizing over the latent variables:

(18.17) 

$$
P r(\mathbf{x}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})=\int Pr(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})d\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}. \quad (18.18)
$$

 To train the model, we maximize the log-likelihood of the training data $\lbracex_{i}\rbrace$ with respect to the parameters $\phi$ : 

$$
\hat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}_{1\ldots T}=\underset{\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T}}{\mathrm{a r g m a x}}\left[\sum_{i=1}^{I}\log\left[P r(\mathbf{x}_{i}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{\mathbf{1}\ldots T})\right]\right]. \quad (18.19)
$$

 We can't maximize this directly because the marginalization in equation 18.18 is intractable. Hence, we use Jensen's inequality to define a lower bound on the likelihood and optimize the parameters $\phi_{1\ldots T}$ with respect to this bound exactly as we did for the VAE (see section 17.3.1).

## 18.4.1 Evidence lower bound (ELBO)

To derive the lower bound, we multiply and divide the log-likelihood by the encoder distribution $q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\mathbf{x})$ and apply Jensen's inequality (see section 17.3.2): 

$$
\begin{align*}\log\left[Pr(\mathbf{x}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})\right]&=\log\left[\int Pr(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})d\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}\right]\\&=\log\left[\int q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\mathbf{x})\frac{Pr(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})}{q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\mathbf{x})}d\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}\right]\\&\geq\int q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\mathbf{x})\log\left[\frac{Pr(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})}{q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\mathbf{x})}\right]d\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}.\end{align*} \quad (18.20)
$$
