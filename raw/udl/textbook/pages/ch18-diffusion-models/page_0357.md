## 18.4 Training

The joint distribution of the observed variable $\mathbf{x}$ and the latent variables $\lbrace \mathbf{z}_{t}\rbrace$ is:

$$
\Pr(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}\,|\,\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})=\Pr(\mathbf{x}\,|\,\mathbf{z}_{1},\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1})\prod_{t=2}^{T}\Pr(\mathbf{z}_{t-1}\,|\,\mathbf{z}_{t},\boldsymbol{\phi}_{t})\cdot \Pr(\mathbf{z}_{T})
\qquad (18.17)
$$

The likelihood of the observed data $\Pr(\mathbf{x}\,|\,\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})$ is found by marginalizing over the latent variables:


$$
\Pr(\mathbf{x}\,|\,\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})=\int \Pr(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}\,|\,\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})d\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}
\qquad (18.18)
$$

To train the model, we maximize the log-likelihood of the training data $\lbrace \mathbf{x}_{i}\rbrace$ with respect to the parameters $\boldsymbol{\phi}$:

$$
\hat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}_{1\ldots T}=\underset{\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T}}{\mathop{\mathrm{arg\,max}}}\left[\sum_{i=1}^{I}\log\left[\Pr(\mathbf{x}_{i}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})\right]\right]
\qquad (18.19)
$$

We can't maximize this directly because the marginalization in equation 18.18 is intractable. Hence, we use Jensen's inequality to define a lower bound on the likelihood and optimize the parameters $\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T}$  with respect to this bound exactly as we did for the VAE (see section 17.3.1).

## 18.4.1 Evidence lower bound (ELBO)

To derive the lower bound, we multiply and divide the log-likelihood by the encoder distribution  $q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}\,|\,\mathbf{x})$  and apply Jensen's inequality (see section 17.3.2):

$$
\begin{aligned}
\log\left[\Pr(\mathbf{x}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})\right]&=\log\left[\int \Pr(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})d\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}\right]\\
&=\log\left[\int q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\mathbf{x})\frac{\Pr(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})}{q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\mathbf{x})}d\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}\right]\\
&\geq\int q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\mathbf{x})\log\left[\frac{\Pr(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\boldsymbol{\phi}_{1\ldots T})}{q(\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}|\mathbf{x})}\right]d\mathbf{z}_{1\ldots T}.
\end{aligned}
\qquad (18.20)
$$
