We continue in this way, computing the derivatives of the output with respect to these intermediate quantities (figure 7.4):

$$
\begin{aligned}\frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial f_{2}}&=\quad\frac{\partial h_{3}}{\partial f_{2}}\left(\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial h_{3}}\frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial f_{3}}\right)\\ \frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial h_{2}}&=\quad\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial h_{2}}\left(\frac{\partial h_{3}}{\partial f_{2}}\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial\ell_{i}\frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial f_{3}}}\right)\\ \frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial f_{1}}&=\quad\frac{\partial h_{2}}{\partial f_{1}}\left(\frac{\partial f_{2}}{\partial\ell_{i}\frac{\partial h_{3}}{\partial f_{3}}\frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial f_{2}}}\right)\\ \frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial h_{1}}&=\quad\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial h_{1}}\left(\frac{\partial h_{2}}{\partial\ell_{i}\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial f_{1}}\frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial h_{2}}}\right)\\ \frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial f_{0}}&=\quad\frac{\partial h_{1}}{\partial f_{0}}\left(\frac{\partial f_{1}}{\partial\ell_{i}\frac{\partial h_{2}}{\partial f_{1}}\frac{\partial f_{3}}{\partial\ell_{i}\frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial h_{2}}}}\right).\\ \end{aligned} \tag{7.13}
$$

In each case, we have already computed the quantities in the brackets in the previous step, and the last term has a simple expression. These equations embody Observation 2 from the previous section (figure 7.2); we can reuse the previously computed derivatives if we calculate them in reverse order.

Backward pass #2: Finally, we consider how the loss $\ell_{i}$ changes when we change the parameters $\lbrace \beta_{k} \rbrace$ and $\lbrace \omega_{k} \rbrace$. Once more, we apply the chain rule (figure 7.5):

$$
\begin{aligned}\frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial\omega_{k}}&=\quad\frac{\partial f_{k}}{\partial\beta_{k}}\frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial f_{k}}.\\ \frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial\omega_{k}}&=\quad\frac{\partial f_{k}}{\partial\beta_{k}}\frac{\partial\ell_{i}}{\partial\omega_{k}}.\end{aligned} \tag{7.14}
$$

In each case, the second term on the right-hand side was computed in equation 7.13. When $k > 0$, we have $\mathbf{f}_{k} = \beta_{k} + \omega_{k} \cdot h_{k}$, so:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{\partial f_{k}}{\partial\beta_{k}}=1\quad\text{and}\quad\frac{\partial f_{k}}{\partial\omega_{k}}\;=\;h_{k}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{7.15}
$$
