Problem 16.5 Consider a random variable z with mean $\mu$ and covariance $\Sigma$ that is transformed as $x = Az + b$. Show that the expected value of x is $\mathbf{A}\mu + \mathbf{b}$ and that the covariance of $x$ is $\mathbf{A}\Sigma\mathbf{A}^{T}$.

Problem 16.6* Prove that if $\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{f}[z] = \mathbf{A}\mathbf{z} + \mathbf{b}$ and $\Pr(\mathbf{z}) = \mathrm{Norm}_{\mathbf{x}}[\mu, \Sigma]$, then $\Pr(\mathbf{x}) = \mathrm{Norm}_{\mathbf{x}}[\mathbf{A}\mu + \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{A}\Sigma\mathbf{A}^{T}]$ using the relation:

$$
\Pr(\mathbf{x})=\Pr(\mathbf{z})\cdot\left|\frac{\partial\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{z}]}{\partial\mathbf{z}}\right|^{-1}
\qquad (16.30)
$$

Problem 16.7 The Leaky ReLU is defined as:

$$
\Pr(\mathbf{x})=\Pr(\mathbf{z})\cdot\left|\frac{\partial\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{z}]}{\partial\mathbf{z}}\right|^{-1}
\qquad (16.30)
$$

Write an expression for the inverse of the leaky ReLU. Write an expression for the inverse absolute determinant of the Jacobian  $\partial[f\mathbf{z}]/\partial z^{-1}$  for an elementwise transformation  $x = f[z]$  of the multivariate variable z where:

$$
\mathbf{f}[\mathbf{z}]=\left[{\mathrm{LeakyReLU}}[z_{1}],\mathbf{LeakyReLU}[z_{2}],\ldots,\mathbf{LeakyReLU}[z_{D}]^{\mathrm{T}}\right.

$$
