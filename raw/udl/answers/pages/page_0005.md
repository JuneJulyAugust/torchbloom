For an intuitive argument to why this is the case, consider fitting a line through

Then we’ll add a third point.
The squared distance to this new point
vertically (discriminative case) will not be the same as the squared distance to this point
horizontally (generative case), and so the new point will change the original line to greater
or lesser degree in the two cases.
In the discriminative case, we assume that the errors are in the output y. In the
generative case, we assume that the errors are in the input, x.
