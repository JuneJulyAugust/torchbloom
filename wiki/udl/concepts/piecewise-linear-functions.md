# Piecewise-Linear Functions

## Core Idea

A piecewise-linear function is made from straight-line pieces. The slope can
change at joints, but each region is still linear.

## Why It Matters In UDL

Chapter 3's ReLU networks produce continuous piecewise-linear functions. This
gives more flexibility than one line while staying visually understandable.

## High-School Bridge

Think of a graph made from connected line segments. Each segment has its own
slope; the places where segments meet are joints.
