# Ch02 Q02: Closed-Form Linear Regression

## Prompt

Use the gradients from ch02-q01, set them to zero, and solve for the line
parameters that minimize least-squares loss.

## What This Tests

Whether "the best parameters" can be connected to "flat slope at the bottom of
the loss surface."

## Hints

- You should get two equations in two unknowns.
- Keep sums like `sum x_i` and `sum y_i` symbolic until the end.
- This closed-form trick is special to simple linear regression.

## Solution Notes

Review needed against source and algebra.

## Self-Check Rubric

- The derivation starts from the gradients.
- Both unknown parameters are solved together.
- The final explanation distinguishes closed-form fitting from iterative training.
